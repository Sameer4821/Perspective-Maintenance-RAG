SECTION 1: SAFETY INSTRUCTIONS
================================================================================

WARNING: Read all instructions before installation or operation.
Failure to follow safety instructions may result in serious injury or death.

1.1 General Safety
- Always isolate the pump from power before performing maintenance
- Wear appropriate PPE including safety glasses and gloves
- Ensure system pressure is zero before opening any connections
- Never operate the pump dry (without liquid)
- Maximum operating temperature: 120°C (248°F)
- Maximum operating pressure: 16 bar (232 PSI)

1.2 Electrical Safety
- Verify power supply matches motor nameplate specifications
- Ensure proper grounding before energizing
- Use lockout/tagout (LOTO) procedures before maintenance

SECTION 3: INSTALLATION PROCEDURE
================================================================================

3.1 Pre-Installation Checks
Step 1: Inspect pump for damage incurred during shipping
Step 2: Verify pump model matches order specifications
Step 3: Check foundation is level to within 0.1mm/m
Step 4: Confirm pipe sizes match pump inlet/outlet connections
Step 5: Verify motor voltage matches available power supply

3.2 Mechanical Installation
Step 1: Position pump on prepared foundation
Step 2: Level pump using precision level (tolerance ±0.05mm/m)
Step 3: Tighten foundation bolts to 45 Nm torque
Step 4: Connect suction piping — minimum straight length: 5x pipe diameter
Step 5: Connect discharge piping — install non-return valve within 500mm
Step 6: Connect pressure gauges at suction and discharge ports
Step 7: Fill seal chamber with flushing liquid if required

3.3 Shaft Alignment
Step 1: Perform initial rough alignment using straight edge
Step 2: Install dial indicators on coupling
Step 3: Rotate coupling and record readings at 0°, 90°, 180°, 270°
Step 4: Adjust motor feet until parallel misalignment < 0.05mm
Step 5: Adjust until angular misalignment < 0.05mm per 100mm
Step 6: Tighten all fasteners and re-verify alignment
Step 7: Record final alignment values in maintenance log

SECTION 4: OPERATION GUIDELINES
================================================================================

4.1 Start-Up Procedure
Step 1: Prime the pump completely — open vent valve until liquid flows
Step 2: Fully open suction valve
Step 3: Set discharge valve to 30% open
Step 4: Energize motor and verify rotation direction
Step 5: Gradually open discharge valve to operating point
Step 6: Check current draw — must not exceed motor nameplate amperage
Step 7: Verify operating vibration < 2.8 mm/s RMS
Step 8: Check for leaks at all connections

4.2 Shutdown Procedure
Step 1: Gradually close discharge valve to minimum flow
Step 2: De-energize motor
Step 3: Close suction valve
Step 4: If freezing conditions expected, drain pump completely

SECTION 5: PREVENTIVE MAINTENANCE SCHEDULE
================================================================================

5.1 Daily Inspections (Every 8 Operating Hours)
- Check bearing temperature — maximum 70°C above ambient
- Check vibration level — maximum 2.8 mm/s RMS
- Inspect mechanical seal for leakage — maximum 10 drops/minute acceptable
- Check suction and discharge pressures
- Verify motor current within nameplate limits

5.2 Weekly Maintenance (Every 50 Operating Hours)
- Inspect coupling condition and alignment
- Check all bolted connections for tightness
- Inspect foundation for cracking or settlement
- Lubricate motor bearings if grease nipples present
- Clean pump exterior and check for corrosion

5.3 Monthly Maintenance (Every 250 Operating Hours)
- Measure and record bearing vibration spectrum
- Check shaft seal adjustment (gland packing only)
- Inspect all gaskets and O-rings for deterioration
- Verify instrumentation calibration (pressure gauges)
- Review operating data trends

5.4 Annual Maintenance (Every 2000 Operating Hours)
- Complete pump disassembly and inspection
- Replace mechanical seal regardless of condition
- Replace bearings regardless of condition
- Measure impeller clearance — replace if > 0.5mm
- Inspect casing for erosion, corrosion, or cracking
- Replace all gaskets and O-rings
- Reassemble and realign pump

5.5 Bearing Replacement Criteria
Replace bearings when ANY of the following occur:
- Vibration velocity exceeds 4.5 mm/s RMS
- Bearing temperature exceeds ambient + 70°C
- Audible noise (rumbling, squealing, or grinding)
- Bearing housing shows blue discoloration (overheating)
- Scheduled 2000-hour replacement regardless of condition

SECTION 6: TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE
================================================================================

6.1 Pump Fails to Start

| Probable Cause              | Corrective Action                                    |
|-----------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| No power supply             | Check fuses, circuit breaker, wiring connections     |
| Motor overload trip         | Reset overload relay; check for mechanical binding   |
| Control panel fault         | Check control panel display for fault codes          |
| Pump seized/locked          | Rotate shaft manually; disassemble if seized         |
| Suction valve closed        | Open suction valve fully                             |

6.2 Pump Not Delivering Required Flow

| Probable Cause              | Corrective Action                                    |
|-----------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| Wrong rotation direction    | Swap any two motor phases; recheck direction         |
| Impeller clogged            | Disassemble and clean impeller passages              |
| Air lock in suction line    | Re-prime pump; check suction line for air ingress   |
| Discharge valve closed      | Open discharge valve to operating position           |
| Impeller worn or damaged    | Measure clearances; replace impeller if worn         |
| Speed too low               | Check motor speed; inspect belt/coupling             |
| Suction lift too high       | Reduce suction lift; install booster pump            |

6.3 Pump Overheating

| Probable Cause              | Corrective Action                                    |
|-----------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| Insufficient flow (min flow)| Ensure flow above minimum: CP-500 = 8 m³/h minimum  |
| Liquid temperature too high | Check process conditions; verify cooling             |
| Bearing failure             | Replace bearings; check lubrication                  |
| Mechanical seal friction    | Replace mechanical seal; check flush flow            |
| Pump running against closed | Never run against closed discharge valve             |
| discharge valve             |                                                      |

6.4 Excessive Vibration

| Probable Cause              | Corrective Action                                    |
|-----------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| Shaft misalignment          | Re-align pump to motor; check coupling               |
| Impeller imbalance          | Inspect impeller for damage; rebalance or replace    |
| Cavitation                  | Increase suction pressure; reduce flow rate          |
| Worn bearings               | Replace bearings; check lubrication schedule         |
| Resonance with pipework     | Add pipe supports; modify support locations          |
| Foundation loose            | Tighten foundation bolts to 45 Nm                    |

6.5 Mechanical Seal Leakage

| Probable Cause              | Corrective Action                                    |
|-----------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| Normal leakage              | < 10 drops/minute is acceptable — no action needed  |
| Seal face worn              | Replace complete mechanical seal assembly            |
| O-ring deteriorated         | Replace seal O-rings with compatible material        |
| Shaft runout excessive      | Check shaft straightness; replace if > 0.05mm TIR   |
| Incorrect seal for fluid    | Verify seal material compatibility with process fluid|

7.1 VFD (Variable Frequency Drive) Fault Codes

| Code  | Description                    | Cause                        | Action                              |
|-------|--------------------------------|------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| E-001 | Overcurrent                    | Motor overloaded             | Reduce speed/load; check motor      |
| E-002 | Overvoltage                    | Supply voltage too high      | Check supply voltage                |
| E-003 | Undervoltage                   | Supply voltage too low       | Check supply; check connections     |
| E-004 | Overtemperature - Drive        | Poor ventilation             | Clean drive vents; check fan        |
| E-005 | Overtemperature - Motor        | Motor overloaded/hot         | Reduce load; check cooling          |
| E-006 | Ground Fault                   | Insulation failure           | Megger test motor; check cables     |
| E-007 | Phase Loss                     | Missing supply phase         | Check fuses and supply connections  |
| E-008 | Communication Fault            | Control signal lost          | Check control wiring; restart       |
| E-009 | Motor Stall                    | Motor cannot accelerate      | Check for mechanical blockage       |
| E-010 | Keypad Communication Error     | Keypad disconnected          | Check keypad connection             |

7.2 Pump Control System Alarm Codes

| Alarm | Description                    | Setpoint          | Action Required                     |
|-------|--------------------------------|-------------------|-------------------------------------|
| A-01  | High Bearing Temperature       | > 85°C            | Stop pump; inspect bearing          |
| A-02  | High Vibration                 | > 4.5 mm/s RMS    | Stop pump; investigate cause        |
| A-03  | Low Suction Pressure           | < 0.5 bar         | Check suction conditions            |
| A-04  | High Discharge Pressure        | > 14 bar          | Check downstream valve position     |
| A-05  | Seal Leak Detected             | Float switch trip | Stop pump; replace mechanical seal  |
| A-06  | Motor High Current             | > 110% FLA        | Reduce load; check motor condition  |
| A-07  | Low Flow Rate                  | < minimum flow    | Check valve positions; check line   |
| A-08  | High Liquid Temperature        | > 95°C            | Check process; inspect cooling      |
